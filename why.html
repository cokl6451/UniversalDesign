Universal Design

Should I Always Design with Accessibility in Mind?

Yes, Here's Why

Taking steps to create an accessible environment isn't just the right AND legal thing to do, it is also the most intelligent business decision. Millions of individuals are affected by disabilities worldwide, and unless there's some medical break through, that number will only increase. For example, in the 2022 census the number of people over 80 years old increased by 25% since the 2016 census. Not only will accessible design allow brands to tap into this audience, it also gives this target audience the opportunity to connect with the world. One of the most important aspects of a society is the ability to work together, and by creating an accessible environment, we are allowing everyone to contribute to the world.

Inclusivity

Universal Design aligns with corporate social responsibility goals by actively removing barriers and creating equitable environments. This commitment to inclusivity and equality reflects positively on your brand, enhancing your company’s reputation. In today’s socially conscious market, companies that prioritize inclusivity in their design philosophy are often viewed more favorably by consumers.

Legal Compliance

Adhering to Universal Design principles helps in complying with various accessibility laws and regulations, such as the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA). This proactive approach can significantly reduce the risk of legal challenges related to accessibility issues. Compliance demonstrates that your company is not only legally conscientious but also committed to serving all community members equitably.

User Experience

Implementing Universal Design principles broadens your company's appeal to a wider audience, including people of all ages, abilities, and backgrounds. This inclusive approach ensures that your products, services, or physical spaces cater to a more extensive customer base, leading to increased satisfaction and loyalty. The more people feel included and considered in your designs, the broader and more diverse your market reach becomes.

Innovation

Adopting Universal Design in your company is not just about accessibility; it’s a step towards pioneering a progressive and inclusive future. This approach reflects a deep understanding of diverse customer needs, showcasing your company as empathetic and forward-thinking. By embracing universal design, you're not only adhering to best practices but also positioning your company as an innovator and leader in inclusive design.
