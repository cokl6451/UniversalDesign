Universal Design

What's the Big Idea?

Find Out

Universal Design is an approach to design that takes the entire spectrum of humanity into consideration. Universal design aims to ensure that products are usable, accessible, and inclusive regardless of one's age, ability, or situation. It's origins can be traced back to the 1950s when architect Ron Mace, who wanted to focus on accessible housing after seeing the influx of veterans with disabilities returning from World War II, coined the term "Universal Design". Some of the most common examples of Universal Design are ramps, automatic doors, and curb cuts. Now we are seeing that movement expand to the digital world. Universal Design can be applied to any product, but is especially important in the context of web design. The web was created to connect and advance humanity, without making this accessible to all, we would be silencing millions of voices and missing out on the potential of millions of minds.
